What is Physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is a regulated healthcare profession focused on restoring movement and function in individuals affected by injury, illness, or disability. Through a combination of hands-on treatment, targeted exercises, and patient education, physiotherapists help people manage pain, recover from injuries, and prevent future problems.

Whether you are dealing with a musculoskeletal condition, recovering from a sports injury, rehabilitating after surgery, managing chronic pain, or looking to prevent injuries before they occur, physiotherapy offers a safe and effective path to improved health. Physiotherapists use evidence-based techniques to address the root cause of your symptoms, not just the symptoms themselves, so you can achieve lasting results.

Jumana's Approach

Jumana Khambatwala takes a holistic, patient-centric approach to physiotherapy. She understands that no two patients are the same, which is why every treatment plan is tailored to your individual needs, goals, and lifestyle. Jumana combines thorough assessment with a range of specialized techniques to deliver comprehensive care that goes beyond symptom management.

Jumana's treatment techniques include:

  • Manual Therapy - including McKenzie and Mulligan Mobilization techniques to restore joint mobility and reduce pain
  • Acupuncture - certified acupuncture treatment for pain relief and tissue healing
  • K Taping - therapeutic taping to support muscles and joints during recovery
  • Exercise Therapy - individualized exercise programs to strengthen, stretch, and improve functional movement
  • Graston Technique - instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilization to break down scar tissue and fascial restrictions
  • Trigger Point Release - targeted treatment of muscle knots and trigger points to relieve pain and tension
  • Active Release Technique - a movement-based soft tissue treatment to address adhesions and improve range of motion

Conditions Treated

Jumana treats a wide range of musculoskeletal and pain conditions, including:

  • Neck and back pain
  • Sports injuries
  • Joint replacements (pre and post-surgical)
  • Shoulder, elbow, wrist, and hand conditions
  • Hip, knee, ankle, and foot conditions
  • Muscle strains and ligament sprains
  • Arthritis and joint stiffness
  • Workplace injuries
  • Motor vehicle accident injuries
  • Chronic pain conditions

What to Expect at Your First Visit

During your initial appointment, Jumana will conduct a thorough assessment to understand your condition, medical history, and personal goals. This includes a detailed evaluation of your movement, strength, flexibility, and posture. Based on her findings, she will develop a personalized treatment plan and begin hands-on treatment during the same session. You will also receive a tailored exercise prescription to continue your recovery at home, along with education on self-management strategies to help you stay on track between appointments.
