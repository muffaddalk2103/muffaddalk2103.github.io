About Jumana

Jumana Khambatwala graduated from D Y Patil College of Physiotherapy in Mumbai, India in April 2012 and practiced there until 2018. In 2019, Jumana moved to Ottawa where she began practicing as a Physiotherapist in good standing with the College of Physiotherapists of Ontario.

Jumana has experience treating patients of all ages with a variety of musculoskeletal conditions, sports injuries including concussion, joint replacements and pelvic health conditions. She is certified in Pelvic Floor Physiotherapy, Acupuncture and Dry Needling. Jumana's physiotherapy assessment and treatment techniques include Manual Therapy like Mckenzie and Mulligan Mobilization, Acupuncture, K Taping, Exercise Therapy, Graston, Trigger point release, Active Release Technique and pelvic floor physiotherapy.

Jumana decided to become a Physiotherapist because of her interest in human anatomy and kinetics and her passion to help people. For her, the most amazing part of being a Physiotherapist is problem solving, clinical reasoning and communication with patients. She believes that since no two patients are ever the same, the approach and treatment for every individual also may not be the same. She is grateful every day in practice for the opportunity to meet, engage with, and help people from all stages of life.

Jumana believes that physiotherapy just doesn't help patients with their ailments but also helps them with their confidence and quality of life. As a Physiotherapist, her goal is always to have a holistic and patient centric approach that will make them feel better and also help them to lead a healthier and fitter lifestyle. When not at work, Jumana enjoys traveling, going on hikes, working out and exploring new places.
